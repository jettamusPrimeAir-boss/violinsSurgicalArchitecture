Candidate: B.S. Biomedical Engineering (Junior), The University of Texas at Austin

Executive Summary

My research focuses on bridging the gap between embedded biomechanical sensors and predictive machine learning models in continuum and soft robotics. By combining high-frequency hardware telemetry with advanced data orchestration, I aim to solve critical state-estimation challenges in surgical robotics, such as mechanical hysteresis, stiction, and complex shape configuration for devices with infinite degrees of freedom.

I. Foundation: Biomechanics & Shape Reconstruction

The ARTS Laboratory, UT Austin

Undergraduate Research Assistant

My foundational research centers on the complex modeling and state estimation of continuum manipulators designed for Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS). Because these systems lack discrete rigid joints, estimating their shape in 3D space requires advanced embedded perception.

  • Sensor Integration Captured and processed high-resolution strain data using Optical Frequency Domain Reflectometry (OFDR) and embedded Fiber Bragg Grating (FBG) sensors along a continuum manipulator backbone.
  • Shape Sensing Assembly (SSA) Mechanized the reconstruction of the manipulator’s shape configuration using VICON Nexus optical sensors and biomarkers to validate internal telemetry.
  • Data Analysis Utilized numerical methods in MATLAB to process 4 datasets (50 trials per dataset), successfully interpolating strain and radial curvature to achieve accurate 3D shape configurations.

II. Core Architecture: The DOPAMINE API

Data Orchestration & Predictive Architecture

Building on my ARTS Lab experience, DOPAMINE is a conceptual data pipeline designed to ingest raw, noisy telemetry from flexible manipulators and process it into clean datasets for training predictive machine learning models.

To handle the mechanical realities of robotic actuation, DOPAMINE integrates concepts inspired by the HERO Lab's JF-OS architecture (used in da Vinci Research Kit optimization):

Telemetry Ingestion
(M.E.O.W. Node)

Acts as a real-time listener for OFDR/FBG strain data, translating raw optical frequencies into a standardized telemetry audit trail. This ensures the data feeding the ML inference engine is pre-validated for sub-millimeter accuracy.

Hysteresis Cleansing
(S.L.I.P. Filter)

To counteract static friction ("stiction") and tendon lag inherent in tendon-driven continuum arms, DOPAMINE applies a digital filter inspired by Stochastic Linearization via Induced Phasing. By applying mathematical dithering to the telemetry, the pipeline isolates and cleanses friction artifacts before model training.

Predictive Inference
(Real-Time Modeling)

Moves beyond static MATLAB interpolation to predict dynamic tendon lag and tool-tip deviation in real-time, functioning as a proactive slip/error control policy.

III. Cross-Domain Scalability & PhD Alignment

Because the mathematical challenges of state estimation in continuum manipulators are highly transferable, the DOPAMINE architecture is designed with M.O.P.E.D.-style modularity (Modular Operating Platform for Engineering & Development). The core inference engine remains static, while the input modules can be hot-swapped to support advanced research targets:

Target A

Surgical Miniaturization

Vanderbilt VISE / MED Lab

Application:

Scaling the OFDR shape-reconstruction algorithms to needle-diameter, tentacle-like continuum robots used in natural orifice and neurological surgeries.

Goal:

Utilizing predictive ML to ensure sub-millimeter tip accuracy even when the manipulator undergoes complex "s-bends" and navigates tortuous anatomical pathways.

Target B

Embedded Perception

UT Austin ACSES / MERGe

Application:

Swapping the rigid FBG optical input module for highly deformable tactile sensors (e.g., stretchable elastomers or fluidic sensors).

Goal:

Applying the same matrix transformations and non-linear interpolations used in surgical snake-lasers to map continuous sensory data into predictive 3D shapes for compliant, human-safe soft robots.
